Connector terminals are typically the conductive parts on the round connector used for power transmission, such as the pins or slots of the connector. However, they can be any conductive terminals between two connectors. Terminal pins or socket contacts are used so that the connector can operate under rated voltage and current specifications, environmental conditions, and specified mechanical and physical loads. All terminals should provide stable connections between terminal insulating layers, and it is recommended to use the same material for terminals on each side of the connector to maintain consistency in specifications. If the connector is not connected with the appropriate and correct connector housing, it is prone to fail prematurely. We offer connector terminals with various characteristics according to application needs. A variety of metals, sizes, lengths, and surface area ratings are available to choose from. Connector terminals are used in our round connectors and are suitable for the following applications: defense, marine, commercial, and industrial operations.
